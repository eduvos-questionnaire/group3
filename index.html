The influence of gender stereotyped and non-stereotyped marketing on the consumer decision making process of Generation Z consumers in Cape Town:

Consent letter

Dear Respondent
We would like to take this opportunity to thank you for your willingness to assist us with acquiring the necessary information for our research. The purpose of this online questionnaire is to obtain information regarding the extent of gender stereotyped and non-stereotyped marketing on the decision making process of Generation Z in Cape Town. The value of this research is that it can provide significant insight to marketing organisations, whether they should adjust their marketing strategy or not.

Respondents must have a general understanding of stereotyped and non- stereotyped marketing campaigns; they must be 18 years or older and will remain anonymous. Participation in the study is voluntary and respondents have the right to withdraw at any time. There are no envisaged risks to respondents.The data collected will be captured personally by the researcher using Google Forms. All information gathered will be confidential.

The report will only be made available to the researchers, supervisor and Eduvos.
Thank you for your time and effort.

Students
Odette du Preez, Nicole Lane and Jolene Smit
